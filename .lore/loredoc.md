# PART I: THE WORLD

## THE ANCIENT WORLD

### I. The Waygates

At the heart of every known world stands a waygate.

#### What They Are

Waygates are colossal archways formed of stone, root, and mineral, often overtaken by natural growth. At a distance, they resemble geological features shaped by erosion or time\u2014ancient cliffs worn smooth, or canyon mouths carved by forgotten rivers.

Only when approached do they reveal intentional structure, as though the land itself briefly remembered how to build.

#### How They Function

**No Interface Exists:**
- No inscriptions have ever been identified on any waygate
- No controls, levers, or mechanisms
- No interfaces or activation sequences
- All known waygates function identically

**Operation:**
- They require no activation
- They require no fuel
- They require no conscious operation
- Travelers pass through them as they would pass beneath an arch or through a canyon

**The Gate's Response:**
The gate responds automatically, reading the traveler's \u00e6theric signature and conveying them onward.

**Unknown Parameters:**
- How the destination is selected remains unknown
- The number of worlds connected by the waygate network has never been determined
- Many worlds are known; many more are suspected
- No comprehensive map exists

**Reliability:**
No recorded instance exists of a waygate malfunctioning.

#### Who Built Them

**The Builder Question:**

No verified record identifies the builders of the waygates. No ruins, tools, or cultural remnants have been conclusively linked to their construction.

It is commonly assumed that the builders are extinct, though no evidence confirms this.

**Scholarly Consensus:**

Scholars widely believe the waygates were not designed as portals in the conventional sense, but as **stabilizing anchors**\u2014structures that regulated adjacency, distance, and orientation between worlds already capable of interacting.

> **Note:** This belief is not proven, but it is consistent with post-catastrophe observations.

### II. The \u00c6ther

Between the worlds lies the \u00e6ther.

#### What It Is

The \u00e6ther is not empty space.

It is an active, hostile medium saturated with energies incompatible with unprotected living matter.

**Primary Property:**
Direct exposure of organic tissue to the \u00e6ther results in **total molecular disintegration**. This effect is immediate and irreversible.

#### The Elven Bride Incident

This property was confirmed beyond theoretical dispute following a historic incident that would reshape interworld politics for generations.

**The Event:**

An interworld vessel experienced a minor breach in its flux field during transit. A single strand of an elven bride's hair crossed the boundary into the \u00e6ther.

The bride died instantly.

**The Consequences:**

1. The death was initially believed to be an assassination
2. An eight-year war erupted between two elven clans
3. The true cause\u2014\u00e6theric exposure\u2014was discovered only later, during an unrelated military disaster
4. Though the war ended soon after the discovery, relations between the involved cultures never fully recovered

**What It Revealed:**

The incident proved definitively that the \u00e6ther does not merely harm. It annihilates. One strand of hair. One moment of exposure. Total death.

#### Fundamental Nature

The \u00e6ther appears indifferent rather than malicious.

It does not seek to destroy life. Rather, life appears fundamentally incompatible with it.

Whether this incompatibility is accidental or intentional remains unknown.

### III. Interworld Travel Beyond the Waygates

Waygates are not the only means of traveling between worlds.

#### The Interworld Vessels

Certain civilizations developed interworld vessels capable of traversing the \u00e6ther directly. These vessels vary widely in size and construction, but all rely on the same principle: **the generation of a sustained flux field**.

**The Magic Reactor:**

At the core of each vessel is a magic reactor. When activated, it projects a protective envelope around the hull, isolating everything inside from the \u00e6ther's destructive effects.

The field must be continuously maintained by trained magic users for the duration of the journey.

**The Risk:**

Failure to sustain the field results in immediate loss of all organic matter aboard.

**The Reality:**

Because of this danger, such travel is rare, resource-intensive, and politically sensitive. Only the most advanced civilizations maintain active fleets. Passage is expensive. Contracts are binding. Accidents are catastrophic.

**What This Means:**

The existence of these vessels demonstrates that the \u00e6ther can be **resisted, but not tamed**. All known methods rely on **containment, not control**.

## THE CATASTROPHE

### IV. The Mining Operation

At some point in the past, a dwarven civilization uncovered and mined a rare crystalline formation later termed **tuning crystals**.

#### What Happened

**Initial State:**
- At the time, the purpose of these crystals was unknown
- The mining operation was successful
- The dwarves extracted the crystals and transported them elsewhere
- The immediate consequences were subtle

**What Did NOT Happen:**
- Waygates continued to function
- No mass extinctions occurred
- No worlds vanished
- No cataclysmic explosion or dimensional collapse

#### The Gradual Unraveling

Instead, gradual anomalies emerged across multiple connected worlds.

**Observable Effects:**
- Landscapes shifted without geological cause
- Ruins appeared where none had stood
- Familiar locations subtly failed to align with memory or record
- Atmospheric and ecological irregularities became increasingly common
- The stars no longer aligned with recorded cycles

**The Pattern:**

At first, these events seemed unrelated\u2014local disturbances, isolated incidents, natural variation. But as reports accumulated across worlds, scholars began to recognize a pattern.

Something systemic was failing.

#### What the Crystals Were

Later scholarship concluded that the tuning crystals were not power sources, but **calibration structures**\u2014components that maintained alignment and synchronization between worlds already linked by the waygate network.

Their removal did not destroy the system.

It degraded it.

#### The True Nature of the Catastrophe

The catastrophe is best understood not as a single event, but as the beginning of an **ongoing condition: desynchronization**.

> Worlds did not break. They drifted.

The effects persist to this day.

---

### V. The Pilots

#### When They Appeared

Pilots began appearing only after the catastrophe.

No known world claims them as native. No historical records describe their existence prior to the event.

#### What Makes Them Different

**\u00c6ther Immunity:**

Pilots can traverse the \u00e6ther without:
- Shielding
- Reactors
- Sustaining rituals

They simply walk through it.

This ability is **unique and unexplained**.

No other known creature, race, or construct possesses this trait.

#### How They Are Perceived

Pilots are regarded everywhere as interlopers\u2014present, functional, but never fully belonging.

**Common Assumptions:**
- They are remnants of the catastrophe itself
- They are constructs or echoes rather than true living beings
- They are somehow connected to the desynchronization

**Unverified Theories:**
- Some believe pilots are survivors of a lost world
- Others claim they are walking fractures in reality
- A few suggest they were intentionally created as a response to the catastrophe

No theory has been proven.

#### What They Can Do

Pilots possess the unique ability to interface with **mechs**\u2014powerful armored constructs that amplify their already unusual capabilities.

This bond between pilot and mech is not fully understood, but it is observed to be:
- Instantaneous
- Intuitive
- Highly effective in combat

Pilots are sought after by factions across the worlds for this reason alone.

#### Why They Matter

In a fractured multiverse where travel is dangerous and resources are contested, pilots represent a strategic advantage.

They are:
- Immune to \u00e6theric exposure
- Capable of operating complex war machines
- Able to navigate between worlds without traditional infrastructure

This makes them valuable. And dangerous.

## THE CURRENT STATE OF THE WORLDS

### VI. The Fragmented Present

The catastrophe did not end the worlds. It scattered them.

#### What Changed

**Before:**
- Worlds existed in stable, predictable alignment
- Travel between them was routine
- Trade, diplomacy, and cultural exchange flourished

**After:**
- Alignment became unstable
- Distances between worlds fluctuate
- Some worlds drift closer; others recede into inaccessibility
- Entire civilizations have been isolated for generations

**The Result:**

The network of connected worlds has fractured into isolated clusters. Some maintain contact through waygates. Others rely on rare and expensive interworld vessels. Many have been cut off entirely.

#### Who Survived

Not all civilizations adapted equally.

**The Dominant Powers:**
- Advanced technological societies with access to interworld vessels
- Magic-rich cultures capable of sustaining protective fields
- Militarized factions that seized control of waygate access

**The Struggling:**
- Worlds without waygate access
- Civilizations dependent on trade routes that no longer function
- Populations stranded on drifting worlds

**The Lost:**
- Entire worlds whose waygates have ceased responding
- Civilizations that vanished during the early chaos
- Cultures erased by ecological collapse or factional war

#### The New Order

In the absence of stable connection, power consolidates around those who control:
- Waygate access
- Interworld vessels
- Strategic resources
- Pilot recruitment

Factions rise and fall. Alliances shift. War is constant.

---

### VII. Camelot: A World in Fracture

One of the most studied post-catastrophe worlds is **Camelot**.

#### What It Was

Before the catastrophe, Camelot was a thriving center of magic and governance. It hosted one of the largest and most stable waygates in the network, serving as a nexus for interworld diplomacy and trade.

#### What It Became

Following the catastrophe, Camelot experienced severe desynchronization.

**Observable Effects:**
- Entire regions phased in and out of alignment with the rest of the world
- Cities flickered between states of ruin and restoration
- The ruling authority collapsed as communication between territories became impossible
- Factional warfare erupted over control of the waygate

**Current State:**

Camelot is now a fractured battlefield. Multiple factions claim authority. The waygate remains functional, but access is contested. Pilots are recruited aggressively by all sides.

Scholars study Camelot as a microcosm of the larger multiverse: a world caught between what it was and what it might become.

# PART II: GAME SYSTEMS

## COMBAT ROLES & TRAIT LINES

### Core Design Philosophy

**Project Orion** employs a role-based combat system where each pilot specializes in a **Trait Line** that defines their battlefield identity. Unlike traditional class systems, trait lines are flexible frameworks that shape playstyle through resource prioritization and feat selection.

**Core Principles:**
- **Role Identity** \u2014 Each trait line has a clear battlefield purpose
- **Resource Dynamics** \u2014 Roles interpret Heat, Energy, and Threat differently
- **Feat Progression** \u2014 Five tiers of feats unlock specialized capabilities
- **Build Flexibility** \u2014 Multiple viable builds within each trait line

## RESOURCE SYSTEM

Resources in **Project Orion** define the mech's behavioral identity. While **structural stats** set physical limits, resources determine how builds *feel* in combat.

### Core Resources

#### \ud83d\udd25 Heat

Heat governs cadence, reactor strain, and overdrive potential.

**Mechanics:**
- Builds with weapon use and reactor strain
- Dissipates gradually when not actively generating
- Reaching max heat causes **reactor overload** (forced cooldown)

**Playstyle Impact:**
- **High Heat Tolerance** \u2014 Burst damage specialists, risk-reward gameplay
- **Low Heat Management** \u2014 Sustained DPS, safe operation
- **Heat Manipulation** \u2014 Builds that weaponize heat levels

**Role Interpretations:**
- **Warrior:** Resource to spend for damage
- **Guardian:** Risk to manage while maintaining threat
- **Marauder:** Fuel for offensive power spikes

---

#### \ud83d\udd0b Energy

Energy governs reactor pool, regeneration, and subsystem uptime.

**Mechanics:**
- Passive regeneration over time
- Consumed by active abilities and sustained systems
- Running out causes **energy starvation** (reduced capabilities)

**Playstyle Impact:**
- **High Energy Pool** \u2014 Sustained ability usage, always active
- **Low Energy Economy** \u2014 Careful resource management, burst windows
- **Energy Regen Focus** \u2014 Near-infinite uptime on key systems

**Role Interpretations:**
- **Warrior:** Secondary sustain for mobility and burst
- **Guardian:** Primary resource for shields and healing
- **Marauder:** Tertiary resource, low priority

---

#### \ud83c\udfaf Threat

Threat governs enemy awareness, aggro generation, and battlefield presence.

**Mechanics:**
- Increases with damage dealt and defensive actions
- Decreases gradually when not engaging
- High threat draws enemy focus

**Playstyle Impact:**
- **High Threat** \u2014 Tank role, enemy magnet
- **Low Threat** \u2014 Stealth striker, avoids focus
- **Threat Manipulation** \u2014 Tactical control over enemy targeting

**Role Interpretations:**
- **Warrior:** Byproduct of damage, managed to avoid focus
- **Guardian:** Primary tool for protecting allies
- **Marauder:** Natural consequence of aggressive playstyle

---

### Resource Priority Chains

Each role prioritizes resources differently, creating distinct playstyle identities.

**Warrior \u2014 Heat > Energy > Threat**
- Heat fuels damage output
- Energy sustains mobility and burst windows
- Threat emerges as consequence, must be managed

**Guardian \u2014 Threat > Energy > Heat**
- Threat is primary tool for protecting allies
- Energy sustains defensive abilities
- Heat is managed to avoid overload

**Marauder \u2014 Heat = Threat > Energy**
- Heat and Threat both fuel offensive pressure
- Natural synergy between damage and aggro
- Energy is minimal concern

## STRUCTURAL STATS

Structural stats define the physical limits and capabilities of the mech chassis.

### Primary Stats

#### Armor
**Function:** Damage reduction and durability  
**Impact:** Determines how much punishment the mech can absorb  
**Scaling:** Increases with chassis tier and armor components

#### Hull Points (HP)
**Function:** Total health pool  
**Impact:** Determines survival threshold  
**Scaling:** Base value from chassis, bonuses from components

#### Mobility
**Function:** Movement speed and dash capability  
**Impact:** Positioning and kiting ability  
**Scaling:** Influenced by leg components and weight distribution

#### Load Capacity
**Function:** Maximum equipment weight  
**Impact:** Limits what can be equipped simultaneously  
**Scaling:** Set by chassis, modified by frame components

---

### Derived Stats

#### Stability
**Function:** Resistance to knockback and stagger  
**Impact:** Determines ability to maintain position under fire  
**Derived From:** Armor + Weight

#### Sensor Range
**Function:** Detection radius for enemies and objectives  
**Impact:** Awareness and tactical information  
**Derived From:** Sensor components and chassis type

#### Heat Capacity
**Function:** Maximum heat before overload  
**Impact:** Determines burst potential vs sustained output  
**Derived From:** Reactor + Cooling Systems

#### Energy Pool
**Function:** Maximum stored energy  
**Impact:** Ability uptime and sustained performance  
**Derived From:** Generator + Capacitors
